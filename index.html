Credits & rollover

Your system generates power during the day. Excess energy can be sent to the grid for credits, which you can use later when production is lower. Unused credits can roll over.

How the program works

Solar panels generate electricity primarily during daylight hours. When your home produces more than it uses, that excess can be exported to the grid and tracked as credits. Those credits can offset usage when the system is producing less — evenings, cloudy periods, or higher-usage seasons.

Simple idea: produce in the day, use credits later — with a smoother bill over time.

Cost structure

Many homeowners explore options with no upfront cost. Instead of paying a utility rate that can change over time, you may pay a lower, fixed rate for the electricity your panels produce — adding predictability and protection against future changes.
